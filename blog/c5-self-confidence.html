May 5th - Self awareness leads to self confidence

The relationship between mindfulness and self awareness

The writer of Search Inside Yourself argues that these are the same thing. But earlier in the chapter he briefly mentions using mindfulness techniques to help his self awareness and boost his self confidence - I think this is closer to the truth. Mindfulness is tool - perhaps the tool - that brings self awareness. It allows you to observe your emotions, thoughts, preferences, actions, and so on without judgement. You can then mindfully assess these characteristics - what they mean to you; how they affect you; how you can change or control them. This is self awareness. With enough self awareness, you can strengthen your mindfulness and the two become ever more symbiotic.

My experience of Body Scan and Journaling

I already have some experience of these things. When I first took and interest in meditation, I read a short book called "With each and every breath" that described a technique similar to Body Scan. I've incorporated it into my daily meditation as a means to get "into the zone". The emphasis in the book I read earlier was to observe the body sensations as a means to feeling your own breath energy, whereas Search Inside Yourself suggests using it to become better aware of your emotions and the effect on your physical self. This is an interesting idea and I look forward to using it outside of a meditation setting.

Journaling is basically keeping a diary isn't it? I used to do that, but I came across a few stumbling blocks: the inability for my writing to keep up with my thoughts; the tendency to self-edit as I go and therefore losing my train of thought; and the stark reality of your rawest thoughts being committed to paper - where you cannot ignore them - is intimidating. I also tend to overwrite, as anyone who's actually read these blogs might have figured out. It can be exhausting!

What I got from "Emotions are Not Me"

This was the most interesting bit, so it's a real shame it was covered so quickly. The subtle but crucial shift in thought from "I am this emotion", to "I feel this emotion" combined the cloud analogy is the real takeaway from this chapter; I look forward to being mindful of this truth.
